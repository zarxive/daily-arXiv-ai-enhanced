Method: 使用复变函数分析方法，将问题转化为斯托克斯流问题，导出通解并引入等周约束进行数值验证。

Result: 理论分析虽显示刚性解，但数值结果表明存在非平凡解，验证了线性解的适用性。

Conclusion: 尽管理论上压力分布不变形，但实际场景可能需要非线性分析，线性解仍可作为设计参考。

Abstract: A linear elastic circular disc is analyzed under a self-equilibrated system
of loads applied along its boundary. A distinctive feature of the
investigation, conducted using complex variable analysis, is the assumption
that the material is incompressible (in its linearized approximation),
rendering the governing equations formally identical to those of Stokes flow in
viscous fluids. After deriving a general solution to the problem, an
isoperimetric constraint is introduced at the boundary to enforce
inextensibility. This effect can be physically realized, for example, by
attaching an inextensible elastic rod with negligible bending stiffness to the
perimeter. Although the combined imposition of material incompressibility and
boundary inextensibility theoretically prevents any deformation of the disc, it
is shown that the problem still admits non-trivial solutions. This apparent
paradox is resolved by recognizing the approximations inherent in the
linearized theory, as confirmed by a geometrically nonlinear numerical
analysis. Nonetheless, the linear solution retains significance: it may
represent a valid stress distribution within a rigid system and can identify
critical conditions of interest for design applications.
